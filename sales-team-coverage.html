How sales teams are organised

Although it depends on a firm’s size, sales teams are usually organised around the two main client groups — institutional and intermediary — and/or geographic area.

Salespeople in each team cover relationships with key individuals in their area of focus. When coverage involves geographic area, this is usually based on the location of specific contacts, not their firm.

Sometimes, a dedicated strategic accounts team covers the firm's most important clients, which are identified at an account (or firm) level.

To help fill any gaps in coverage, client service or sales support teams might cover relationships with contacts who are located in a geographic area that isn't covered by existing sales teams or salespeople.
