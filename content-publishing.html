Content Publishing

In this chapter

Investment managers publish content on a range of topics (e.g., interest rates, inflation, valuations, investment principles, portfolio holdings) in multiple formats (website articles, PDFs, videos, and podcasts).

By producing content the target audience finds useful — without pushing products — a firm can demonstrate expertise, establish credibility, attract new audiences, and build trust with subscribers.

This chapter shares a few ideas around best practice.

Optimal format

In my experience, short-form written content performs best, because it:

  • Reflects how most website visitors consume content (i.e., short attention spans, scanning behaviour)
  • Can cover key points and link to deeper material if needed
  • Requires less resources than other formats (e.g., video production)

If your firm publishes long articles on its website — which investment managers like to do — review the data to check if they are being read. If they aren’t, consider:

  • Replacing the full text with a short summary
  • Making the complete article available as a downloadable PDF
  • Encouraging authors to write shorter, blog-style content by default

Engagement levels for podcasts and other resource-heavy formats should also be looked at. Often, they are introduced because it’s the “done thing” rather than from audience demand, and firms continue trying to force their success despite evidence of limited traction.

Obviously, the ability to enact change will depend on the wider team’s attitude.
