The core marketing content produced by investment managers is the range of materials for the firm’s products and strategies.

Although these materials might be considered somewhat old-fashioned — I've seen lots of empty talk around making them more “engaging” and “interactive” — in my opinion these documents (mostly PDFs) largely deliver what investors want.

Because they are so key — as well as being used in marketing campaigns and online, the firm’s salespeople share these materials with contacts — it’s vital that the sales team have full visibility into what is available and that any downloads by tracked contacts can be reported back to their assigned salesperson.

In this chapter, we collate marketing materials, set up download tracking, and create an internal document library.

Note that for all following tasks in this chapter, you will need to incorporate materials published in any of the additional languages documented in the communication languages part of firm research.

Collating materials

The first step is to determine what document types are produced for individual products and strategies. Common examples include:

  • Factsheets — periodic (monthly or quarterly) updates on portfolio holdings and investment performance
  • Pitchbooks/presentations — detailed documents that cover the investment philosophy, process, people, performance, and the investment case
  • Flyers — a short narrative document, usually around 1 or 2 pages, with key content taken from a relevant pitchbook/presentation
  • Commentaries — periodic commentary (monthly, quarterly, or annually) on performance, attribution, changes to portfolio holdings, and outlook

Most of these materials are produced on a one-to-one basis: a single document for each investment strategy or product. For pooled funds, however, some documents — such as the factsheet — are typically published separately for each share class.

Task

Identify which document types are produced for your firm’s products and strategies. Only include strategy-level materials if was confirmed that your firm offers SMAs as part of firm research. Note if any document types are produced at the share class level.

Often, due to resource constraints, not every strategy or product will have the full set of documents. The next step, therefore, is to note which specific documents are available for each strategy and product.

Setting up download tracking

Once the available materials have been identified, the next step is to make sure they are hosted properly and to ensure any downloads can be tracked in the marketing automation system.

Marketing materials may be stored and hosted on a variety of document management systems or with digital asset management software.

Regardless of which specific system or software your firm uses for hosting documents, we only have a single — but important — requirement: each document should be available on a static URL, which always links to the latest version, doesn't change when the document is updated, and is publicly available.

It should also be clear who is responsible for updating each document, to ensure they stay up-to-date.
