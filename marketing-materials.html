The most important marketing content produced by investment managers is the range of materials for the firm’s products and strategies.

Although these materials — which are mostly PDFs — might be considered somewhat old-fashioned, in my opinion they largely deliver what the firm’s audience needs to analyse investments.

Because they are so key — as well as being used in marketing campaigns and online, salespeople share these materials with their contacts — the sales team should be given full visibility into what is available and be made aware of any downloads by their assigned contacts (whose digital activity is being tracked by the marketing automation system).

In this chapter, we collate marketing materials, set up download tracking, and create an internal document library.

Collating materials

The first step is to determine what types of document are produced for individual products and strategies. Common examples include:

  • Factsheets — periodic updates (monthly or quarterly) on investment performance and portfolio holdings
  • Pitchbooks/presentations — in-depth documents covering investment philosophy, process, people, performance, and the investment case
  • Flyers — a short narrative document, usually around 1 or 2 pages, with key content taken from a relevant pitchbook/presentation
  • Commentaries — periodic commentary (monthly, quarterly, or annually) on performance, attribution, changes to portfolio holdings, and outlook

Most of these materials are produced on a one-to-one basis: a single document for each investment strategy or product. For pooled funds, however, some documents — such as the factsheet — are typically published separately for each share class. Document types may vary for each set of strategies and fund ranges.

Often, due to resource constraints, not every strategy or product will have the full set of documents. The next step, therefore, is to note which specific documents are available for each strategy and product.

To help, a matrix document can be created with document types in the column headers and product/strategies in the row headers, as shown in figure 1. An initial list can be created for strategies (if appropriate) and the process can then be repeated for each product range as needed.

Once all product and strategy materials have been collated, the final step is to identify and note down any other materials you think are relevant — a corporate brochure or presentation, firm factsheet, fund range brochures, regulatory documents, and so on.

Task

Collate relevant marketing materials by:

  • Listing which document types exist for products and strategies, noting if any are produced at the share class level
  • Identify which specific documents are available for each individual product and strategy (use the matrix document above if helpful)
  • Identify and list any other documents you consider relevant

If the firm publishes materials in multiple languages, these will need to be included.

Setting up download tracking

With all materials identified, the next step is to ensure sure any downloads can be tracked by the marketing automation system and attributed to tracked contacts — otherwise, they remain anonymous.

Document hosting

Materials are usually hosted in a document management system or, in some cases, externally (when factsheet production is outsourced, for example). If they are hosted directly in the marketing automation system, this step can be skipped, as tracking should already be in place.

Some document management systems are overly complex — requiring unnecessary tagging and maintenance — but for our needs there is only one requirement: each document should be available on a static, publicly available URL. This should always link to the latest version of the document and not change when new versions are uploaded.

Task

Ensure the document management system hosts documents as needed.

Redirect URLs

Redirect URLs are then created in the marketing automation system for each document. When clicked on, these links redirect to the original hosted document URL — without the downloader noticing anything different — so the marketing automation system can track the download. If the contact is being tracked, the download is assigned to them and logged on their contact record.

Another benefit of redirect URLs is the ability to customise the link to make it more clear and memorable — which is helpful when the document management system uses long, complicated URLs.

For example, a factsheet for a US Equity Fund may be hosted on the following link in the firm’s document management system:

https://documents.lam.com/api/documents/view?documentid=JHysSSjuS_22

It’s not obvious — from looking at the URL — what it links to, but the redirect link can be customised to make this clear:

https://www2.lam.com/documents/lam-us-equity-fund-factsheet-a-acc-usd.pdf

As a final step, all document links on the corporate website should be replaced with the redirect URLs. Once complete, anyone who downloads materials via the new redirect URLs will access the document as normal, but the download will attributed to any tracked contacts and logged on their contact record.

Task

Create redirect links for all marketing materials and replace the original document links on any public-facing websites.

Creating an internal document library

With all documents collated and download tracking in place, the final step is to create an internal document library. It’s primary function is to allow salespeople to easily and quickly find the materials they need, but colleagues in other teams may also find it useful.

As well as allowing colleagues to quickly access what they need, salespeople can share the links on the page with their contacts (rather than, say, attaching the PDF to an Outlook email). If the contact is being tracked, any downloads will be logged on their contact record.

The page should contain links to all available marketing materials in a clear, structured format. Make sure the redirect URLs are used on the page, not the original links. The page can be hosted in the marketing automation system or on the intranet.>
