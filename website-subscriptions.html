Website Subscriptions

In this chapter

Offering website subscriptions allows visitors to choose topics of interest, submit their contact details, and receive notifications when new content is published — this chapter covers how to set them up.

Subscription options

The first step is to decide which subscription options to offer. Fortunately, the category list created for website content (ideally asset classes that align with the firm’s product range) can also form the set of options.

Each of the firm’s products and strategies can be added as additional subscription options, but this might become unwieldy — especially for a large product range. And to make it even more complicated, a subscription to a UCITS fund can only be offered to visitors in countries where the fund is registered.

However, if the initial set of options has been created as a list of asset classes that aligns with the firm's product range, website visitors can be given a simpler choice for product and strategy subscriptions:

Would you like to receive content about (1) strategies and (2) products that align with your asset class selections?

In this case:

  • Asset classes are used for blog subscriptions (non-product content)
  • Product and strategy content is distributed to subscribers who have opted in to general product/strategy updates and a relevant asset class
  • Product content is also restricted to subscribers in countries where the product is registered

Task

Create a set of subscription options.
